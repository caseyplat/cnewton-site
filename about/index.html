Casey Newton

A biography, press photo, and contact information.

// BIOGRAPHY

Casey Newton is the founder and editor of Platformer, an independent publication about the intersection of technology and democracy. Since its launch in 2020, Platformer has been trusted by more than 200,000 weekly readers for its reporting and analysis on AI, technology platforms, and power.

Newton is also the co-host of Hard Fork, a podcast from The New York Times about the future that’s already here, which he hosts with Kevin Roose. Time has named Hard Fork one of the 100 best podcasts of all time.

Before founding Platformer, Newton was the longtime Silicon Valley editor of The Verge, where he covered the rise of social platforms and their growing influence on public discourse. His reporting has focused on content moderation, platform governance, and the labor practices of tech giants, and his stories about the human cost of building the internet were a finalist for the National Magazine Award.

Across Platformer, Hard Fork, and his personal blog at blog.cnewton.org, Newton writes and podcasts about the intersection of technology, democracy, and AI. He lives in San Francisco with his fiancé.
